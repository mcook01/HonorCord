What is the Honor Cord Project?

It's your school career, made visible. Complete acts of Leadership, Citizenship, Compassion, and Integrity that are verified by a witness, and earn digital badges along the way. Students who earn all four receive a black and gold Tiger Honor Cord to wear at their 8th-grade promotion ceremony.

What You Need to Earn Each Badge

Every act must be verified by a witness: a teacher, parent, staff member, or the person you helped.

Inspire Others10 acts
Leadership

Leaders do not wait to be chosen. They see a need and step up. Earn this badge by taking initiative and inspiring the people around you, whether that is in your classroom, on your team, or out in the community.

✅ Each act needs a signature from a witness: a teacher, parent, staff member, or bystander. Any one person may only verify a single act per badge category. The exception: a representative from an organization such as Boy Scouts, Girl Scouts, a sports team, or a faith community may sign for multiple acts as long as each act is a separate event or activity.
Real-world examples
Organize a school supply drive for students in need
Volunteer to run a station at the PTO Color Run
Lead a team project and make sure everyone has a role
Serve as a captain or team leader in a sport or club activity
Present a solution to a real school problem to staff
Earn a leadership rank or role in Boy Scouts or Girl Scouts
Step in to calm a conflict before it grows
Help organize or run a youth group or faith community event
Serve the Community10 acts
Citizenship

Good citizens make their communities stronger. Show that you take responsibility for the world around you, from your school hallways to your neighborhood streets, through action rather than just attitude.

✅ Each act needs a signature from a witness: a teacher, parent, staff member, or bystander. Any one person may only verify a single act per badge category. The exception: a representative from an organization such as Boy Scouts, Girl Scouts, a sports team, or a faith community may sign for multiple acts as long as each act is a separate event or activity.
Real-world examples
Volunteer at the local food pantry
Help set up, run, or clean up at a PTO event like the Color Run
Help a neighbor with yardwork, snow removal, or groceries
Complete a community service project through Boy Scouts or Girl Scouts
Participate in a park or neighborhood cleanup
Donate clothes, books, or supplies to a local shelter or drive
Volunteer at a faith community food drive or outreach event
Represent your school with pride at a public or community event
Lift Others Up6 acts
Compassion

Compassion means noticing when someone is struggling and choosing to do something about it. It is one of the most powerful forces for change in the world, and one of the simplest things to practice every single day.

✅ Each act needs a signature from the person you helped or an adult who witnessed it. Any one person may only verify a single act per badge category. The exception: a representative from an organization such as Boy Scouts, Girl Scouts, a sports team, or a faith community may sign for multiple acts as long as each act is a separate event or activity.
Real-world examples
Sit with someone eating alone and make them feel welcome
Write encouraging notes for a classmate going through a hard time
Help a teammate or classmate catch up on work they missed
Collect and donate care items for a local homeless shelter
Organize a fundraiser or collection drive for a cause you believe in
Volunteer to help younger athletes, performers, or students at a practice or event
Check in on a neighbor, family friend, or senior who lives alone
Speak up for a classmate who is being treated unfairly
Do What's Right6 acts
Integrity

Integrity means doing the right thing when no one is watching, and also when everyone is. It builds trust, earns respect, and makes you the kind of person others count on. The world needs more of it.

✅ Each act needs a signature from a witness: a teacher, parent, staff member, or bystander. Any one person may only verify a single act per badge category. The exception: a representative from an organization such as Boy Scouts, Girl Scouts, a sports team, or a faith community may sign for multiple acts as long as each act is a separate event or activity.
Real-world examples
Return something valuable you found, like a wallet, phone, or money
Admit a mistake to a teacher and take steps to make it right
Refuse to cheat even when no one would know
Tell the truth in a situation where lying would have been easier
Give credit to a teammate whose idea made the project succeed
Complete all your Scout, sports, or faith community commitments even when it is hard
Follow through on a promise even when it is inconvenient
Call out misinformation or unkind content you see online
